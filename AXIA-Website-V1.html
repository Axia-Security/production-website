The problem, reframed

Every tool sees a slice. None of them renders the verdict.

Your stack maps where sensitive data lives, profiles how a user behaves, and logs what happened. Each one is real, and each one stops short of the one thing that stops an insider: a decision at the moment data moves.

DSPM · the file prior

Categorizes the file

What AXIA is

The in-tenant verdict engine.

One engine judges WHO is moving WHAT to WHOM, content sensitivity plus identity and behavior plus destination, into a single explainable verdict. Channels are adapters. Your data never leaves your tenant.

A millisecond Gatekeeper flags data movement; a near real-time Judge renders a verdict with its reasoning. Every channel. In your tenant.

Owns the action

Others open cases. AXIA drives closing them.

Every verdict carries a next-best-action, the proportionate response to this specific move. Where you have authorized it in Settings, AXIA executes it automatically; everywhere else it is one approval away. The verdict drives four families of action.

(a) Engagement

Coach, then escalate

Coach the employee, brief the manager, escalate to HR or legal, graduated by severity.

(b) Content action

Hold, or see-then-act

Hold-then-decide on email; remove, quarantine or revoke on collaboration and file channels.

(c) Identity

Step-up or disable

Step-up auth or disable across Entra, Okta and Ping, including agent identities.

(d) Verdict-out

Into your SOC

Push the verdict to SIEM, SOAR and ticketing, the system, the playbooks, the team.

Agentic insider risk

The same gate. A new subject.

AI agents now act for the company, and they move data like people do. AXIA judges agents as insider-risk subjects under the same gate, and lets agents consume the verdict directly before they act.

Same gate, new subject

An agent acting for the company is judged exactly as a person is, and contained through identity, step-up or disable on its agent identity.

Judgment-as-a-service

An agent calls AXIA via API, CLI or MCP, asks whether an action is an insider-risk event, and obeys the explainable verdict before it acts.

agent.ask("export client_book → personal_drive") → VERDICT: insider-risk event · deny + step-up
Reads your stack, doesn't require it

The layer above the tools you already run.

AXIA reads your existing stack as standing priors and combines them with its own judgment of the content, without requiring any of them to function. Run them if you want. Where they end, a case in a queue, AXIA begins, a resolved outcome.

AXIA isn't another IRM or DLP on the shortlist. It is the layer above: it reads their signal as priors, renders the verdict at the gate, and resolves the outcome they only open.

Agentless by design

No kernel. No endpoint agent. Your data stays in your tenant.

AXIA is agentless by deliberate architecture. In-tenant APIs, a browser-only control and opt-in thin sensors give endpoint-adjacent coverage with none of the kernel risk or deployment friction.

No kernel, no admin rights

Nothing to install deep in the endpoint. No laptop-crash liability, no heavyweight infra, no deep agent to maintain.

Browser Guard + thin sensors

A browser-only control covering webmail, GenAI pastes and uploads, plus opt-in capture for AI-coding and desktop-AI tools.

Frictionless rollout

Works-council-friendly, fast to deploy, and your data never leaves your tenant. Agentless is the deployment benefit, not a gap.
