1. Purpose and Scope

Axia Security (“Axia”) maintains a comprehensive Information Security Program designed to protect its systems, operations, and customer environments from unauthorized access, disruption, or misuse.

This document provides an overview of Axia’s information security policies and standards, which are aligned with industry best practices and evolving compliance frameworks, including the SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security, Availability, and Confidentiality).

The scope of this program includes all Axia personnel, systems, and processes supporting product development, operations, and customer engagements.

2. Governance and Risk Management

Axia maintains a structured governance framework for information security, including:

  • Defined roles and responsibilities across executive management, engineering, and operations
  • Integration of security considerations into business and product decision-making
  • Ongoing identification and assessment of risks to systems and operations
  • Security policies and standards are reviewed periodically and updated to reflect changes in the threat landscape, business operations, and regulatory expectations.

Information security governance is owned by Guy Hindi, Chief Product Officer, who serves as the Qualified Individual under the FTC Safeguards Rule §314.4(a)(1). AXIA's §314.4(f) service-provider oversight posture is described in the Compliance Program Charter (available on request).

3. Access Control and Identity Management

Axia enforces access controls based on the principle of least privilege and business need:

  • Role-based access controls (RBAC) are implemented across internal systems
  • Access to sensitive systems and data is restricted and managed
  • Authentication mechanisms are enforced for system access
  • Access rights are reviewed and adjusted as part of operational processes
  • These controls are designed to reduce the risk of unauthorized access and support accountability.

4. Data Protection and Confidentiality

Axia implements controls to protect sensitive and confidential information, including:

  • Secure handling and processing of data in accordance with internal policies
  • Encryption practices aligned with industry standards, where applicable
  • AXIA's deployment supports TLS 1.2+ for data in transit and AES-256-GCM for data at rest at the storage/file-system layer. Specific cryptographic posture per customer engagement is documented in the DPA Schedule A.
  • Data minimization principles to limit unnecessary exposure
  • For customer engagements, Axia’s solution is deployed within the customer's controlled environment — on-premises OR the customer's own cloud tenant, ensuring:
  • Customer data remains within the organization’s environment
  • No transfer of customer data to Axia-managed systems
  • Full customer control over data access and retention
  • This approach aligns with regulatory expectations for data protection in highly regulated industries.

5. Secure Development and Change Management

Security is integrated into Axia’s software development lifecycle:

  • Secure coding practices are followed by engineering teams
  • Testing and validation are conducted prior to deployment
  • Changes to systems and applications are controlled and managed
  • This ensures that security considerations are embedded throughout the development and release process.

6. Incident Response and Security Monitoring

Axia maintains incident response procedures to address security events and operational incidents:

  • Identification and classification of incidents
  • Defined escalation and response protocols
  • Containment, mitigation, and recovery actions
  • Internal coordination and communication procedures
  • These processes support timely and effective handling of incidents while minimizing potential impact.

7. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

Axia maintains Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery frameworks to support operational resilience:

  • Identification of critical business functions and dependencies
  • Defined recovery priorities and procedures
  • Periodic review and validation of continuity plans
  • These capabilities ensure that Axia can continue operations and respond effectively to disruptions.

8. Infrastructure and Deployment Model

Axia’s deployment model is designed to minimize operational and security risk:

  • Customer-facing deployments run within the customer's controlled environment (on-premises or the customer's own cloud tenant)
  • Axia does not operate customer production data environments
  • Customer data is not transmitted outside the customer's controlled environment
  • This architecture reduces third-party risk and aligns with the expectations of financial institutions and other regulated organizations.

9. Third-Party Risk Management

Axia evaluates third-party vendors and service providers based on security and operational considerations. A formal Vendor Risk Register governance framework is contemplated under AXIA's Compliance Program Charter; formalization timing is aligned with the Charter's policy-library review cycle. Current practice includes:

  • Assessment of vendor security practices where applicable
  • Alignment with internal security standards
  • Ongoing monitoring of third-party relationships
  • This approach is intended to ensure that external dependencies do not introduce undue risk as formalization progresses.

10. Employee Security and Awareness

Axia maintains practices to ensure that employees understand and fulfill their security responsibilities:

  • Adherence to internal security policies and guidelines
  • Responsible handling of sensitive information
  • Awareness of security and operational risks
  • These measures support a culture of security across the organization.

11. Compliance and Continuous Improvement

Axia is committed to continuous improvement of its security posture:

  • Alignment with industry best practices and control frameworks
  • Ongoing enhancement of policies, processes, and controls
  • Security practices evolve alongside the company’s growth and the changing threat landscape.

AXIA has engaged a third-party compliance partner (Scytale) and initiated a formal SOC 2 Type 2 readiness program. Completion timing depends on the audit scope, readiness assessment, and external auditor availability, and cannot be committed as a fixed date.

Conclusion

Axia’s Information Security Program provides a structured and risk-based approach to protecting systems, data, and operations. Combined with its in-customer-environment deployment model, Axia enables organizations—particularly in regulated industries such as financial services, to maintain full control over their data while benefiting from advanced security capabilities.
