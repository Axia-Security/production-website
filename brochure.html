Why Current Tools Fail

Insider risk is not just a detection problem. It is a judgment problem. And judgment is exactly what your current tools lack.

Legacy DLP (Symantec, Forcepoint, Microsoft Purview)

DLP was designed to catch keywords and patterns. It blocks or alerts. Nothing in between. The result: 90% false positive rates, hundreds of alerts per day, and an analyst team buried in noise. Your people spend more time dismissing alerts than investigating real threats.

DSPM Platforms (Cyera, Varonis, Sentra)

Data Security Posture Management tools are built to protect data at rest — classifying what you have, where it lives, and who has access to it. That is a valuable problem to solve, but a fundamentally different one. DSPM platforms cannot see data in motion. They have no visibility into what an employee is actually doing with a file right now — sending it, printing it, exfiltrating it. By the time a DSPM tool flags an issue, the data has already left. They are a map of your risk landscape, not a response to it.

AI-Native DLP (CyberHaven, Nightfall, MIND)

AI-native DLP tools are a step forward — better detection, smarter classification, less noise than legacy systems. But they come with a critical flaw: they still require a full rip-and-replace of your existing DLP infrastructure. That means months of deployment, complex migrations, retraining your team, and betting your entire data security program on an unproven vendor. Better detection is not worth the disruption if you cannot get it deployed and you can't close the loop with engagement. Most enterprise security teams simply do not take it.

The industry keeps building better locks. The problem was never the locks.
