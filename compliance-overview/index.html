AXIA Security & Compliance Overview

Owner: Compliance Officer (AXIA Legal) · Governing decision: DR-2026-107 (certification-claim posture) Jurisdiction: Neutral — this overview serves both EU/UK/Swiss and US prospects. Jurisdiction-specific citations live in the per-engagement questionnaire response, not here.

AXIA at a glance

AXIA is an AI-powered insider-risk platform. AXIA is designed so that customer data stays under the customer's own control: AXIA does not custody customer production data. The sections below summarize AXIA's security posture, data-handling model, and the compliance frameworks AXIA is designed with reference to.

Deployment & data custody

AXIA's platform runs inside the customer's own controlled environment — on-premises or the customer's own cloud tenant — and customer data does not leave the customer's custody. The LLM is always provided by the customer: either the customer's own contracted cloud LLM service (e.g., Bedrock/Azure/Vertex) or a customer-managed/approved API to an on-premises model the customer runs. AXIA does not host or provide the LLM, does not operate a SaaS or multi-tenant service, and does not custody customer production data.

Because of this architecture: - Data residency is customer-determined. Customer data resides only in the region(s) of the customer's own environment. - Customer-to-customer segregation is by tenant/physical isolation. Each deployment is a separate environment in the customer's own tenant or infrastructure; there is no shared AXIA multi-tenant datastore holding multiple customers' production data. - AXIA personnel hold no standing access path to customer production data. Any support performed inside a customer environment occurs under the customer's own controls and logging. - The cloud and model providers are the customer's direct counterparties, not AXIA sub-processors, because the customer contracts them directly within its own environment.

Security program highlights

  • Encryption. TLS 1.2 or higher in transit; AES-256-GCM at rest at the storage/file-system layer (specific cryptographic posture per engagement is set in the Data Processing Addendum).
  • Access control. Role-based access control and least privilege across internal systems, with periodic access reviews.
  • Authentication. Users authenticate through the customer's own identity provider via SSO; the customer's MFA policy applies at login.
  • Monitoring & incident response. Monitoring and detection for AXIA's own systems under a Computer Incident Response Program and Incident Response Policy; in customer deployments the product emits logs into the customer's own security tooling. AXIA commits to prompt incident notification to customers per its contractual terms.
  • Resilience. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery plans with defined recovery objectives and periodic review.
  • Secure development & third-party risk. A secure software development lifecycle, and a Third-Party Risk Management process governed by a Vendor Risk Register that inventories vendors by data-access scope, criticality, certification status, and contractual flow-down.

AI governance

AXIA's AI posture is designed with reference to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0 and the Generative AI Profile, documented in an AXIA self-assessed crosswalk that maps AXIA's controls to the framework's Govern / Map / Measure / Manage functions. This crosswalk is a self-assessment and mapping, not a conformance attestation or certification. AXIA's design includes data minimization (no ingestion of protected-class attributes or HR records), human-confirmation gates on AI-suggested actions, explainability for every alert, and model documentation (model cards).

Compliance frameworks & status

AXIA is designed with reference to the frameworks below. AXIA holds no independent third-party attestation at this time; references are to design targets, not certifications.

  • SOC 2 Type II — readiness in progress. AXIA has engaged a third-party compliance partner (Scytale) and initiated a formal SOC 2 Type II readiness program. No SOC 2 attestation is held, and completion is not committed to a fixed date. The report will be made available to customers under NDA once issued.
  • ISO/IEC 27001 — designed with reference to. AXIA's information security program is designed with reference to ISO/IEC 27001 control families. AXIA is not ISO 27001 certified.
  • GDPR / UK GDPR / Swiss FADP (Art. 32-style security of processing) — designed with reference to, with the in-customer-environment model supporting the customer's own data-protection obligations.
  • FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR 314.4) — service-provider posture. For US financial-services customers, AXIA maintains a self-attested service-provider posture for handling nonpublic personal information. AXIA is not a financial institution for GLBA privacy-notice purposes.
  • NIST AI RMF 1.0 + Generative AI Profile — designed with reference to (self-assessed crosswalk, see AI governance above).

Detailed self-assessment documents (program charter, crosswalk, vendor risk register, supply-chain posture, policy highlights, model cards) and, once issued, the SOC 2 readiness letter and report, are available to customers and prospects under NDA on request.
