Deployment & data custody

AXIA runs inside the customer's own controlled environment — on-premises or the customer's own cloud tenant — and customer data does not leave the customer's custody.

The LLM used for severity judgment is always provided by the customer: either the customer's own contracted cloud LLM service (e.g. Amazon Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, Google Vertex) or a customer-managed/approved API to an on-premises model. AXIA does not host or provide the LLM, does not operate a SaaS or multi-tenant service, and does not custody customer production data.

Data residency

Customer-determined — data resides only in the region(s) of the customer's own environment.

Segregation

Each deployment is a separate environment in the customer's own tenant; no shared multi-tenant datastore.

Provider relationships

The cloud and model providers are the customer's direct counterparties — not AXIA sub-processors.

Personnel access

AXIA personnel hold no standing access path to customer production data; support runs under the customer's controls and logging.

Security program

  • Encryption. TLS 1.2+ in transit; AES-256-GCM at rest at the storage layer.
  • Authentication & access. Users authenticate via the customer's own identity provider (SSO; OIDC+PKCE / SAML 2.0); the customer's MFA policy applies. Per-screen, server-side role-based access control with object-level checks.
  • Audit trail. No state-changing action is recorded without a resolved authenticated principal; AI actions trace to the authorizing human.
  • Monitoring & incident response. Monitoring of AXIA's own systems under a Computer Incident Response Program; the product emits logs into the customer's own security tooling. AXIA notifies the customer within 24 hours of confirming an AXIA-side security incident affecting customer data, followed by a root-cause analysis within 30 days of resolution.
  • Resilience. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery plans with defined recovery objectives and periodic review.
  • Secure development & third-party risk. A secure SDLC and a Vendor Risk Register inventorying vendors by data-access scope, criticality, certification status, and contractual flow-down.

AI governance

AXIA's AI posture is designed with reference to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0 and the Generative AI Profile, documented in an AXIA self-assessed crosswalk (a mapping, not a conformance attestation). The design includes data minimization (no protected-class attributes or HR records), human-confirmation gates on AI-suggested actions, explainability for every alert, and model documentation (model cards).

Compliance & frameworks

AXIA holds no independent third-party attestation at this time. References below are design targets, not certifications.

  • SOC 2 Type II   Readiness in progress. No attestation is held; no completion date is committed. Report shared under NDA once issued.
  • ISO/IEC 27001   Designed with reference to the control families. Not ISO 27001 certified.
  • GDPR / UK GDPR / Swiss FADP   Designed with reference to Article 32-style security of processing; the in-environment model supports the customer's own obligations.
  • NIST AI RMF 1.0 + GenAI Profile   Self-assessed crosswalk (see AI governance).
